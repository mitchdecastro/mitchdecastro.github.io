MGC Portfolio Review

Research / Prototyping / Web Development / Independent Study

For as long as the Michael Graves College has been around, it hasn't quite built up the reputation it deserves. For an independent study, I decided to pursue a means of putting the school on the map by highlighting the talent that emerges from the annual senior portfolio review. Starting out as a mobile app concept, the project has since evolved into an authentic and collaborative effort between brand designer and colleague, Perry Capelakos, and myself.

The initial phase of the project involved a rigorous user-testing process, where I interviewed fellow students & faculty and had them interact with a Marvel prototype of the experience. Specifically-worded instructions and careful observations led me to design revisions and a second round of user-testing with a completely different sample of participants.

While the method of development had changed, the design remained as a user-tested solution from the initial round.

Visual design and branding are currently at the hands of Perry Capelakos. Meanwhile, I am handling the entire development process; approaching the project as a responsive website as opposed to a native app to play to my strengths, save time, and keep it consistent across platforms without any unnecessary overhead.
